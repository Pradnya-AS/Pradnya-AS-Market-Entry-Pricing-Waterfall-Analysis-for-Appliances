## Key Insights

- Vietnam offers the lowest landed cost due to favorable tariff structure  
- India incurs higher final pricing due to higher import duties  
- Mexico, despite lower tariffs, remains expensive due to higher factory cost  

- Amazon channel results in higher consumer prices driven by platform fees (~15%)  
- Retail channel delivers the most competitive pricing due to minimal overhead costs  
- D2C channel has the highest pricing due to elevated marketing spend and higher target margins  

- Channel economics significantly impact final pricing, sometimes more than sourcing cost  

- Best combinations:
  - Lowest price → Vietnam + Retail  
  - Highest margin potential → D2C  
  - Fastest scale → Amazon  

---

## How to Use

- Compare final prices across all combinations  
- Adjust assumptions (tariffs, freight, margins)  
- Identify optimal sourcing + channel strategy

## Summary

This model simulates real-world pricing decisions by combining landed cost, channel margins, and pricing strategy across multiple markets.
